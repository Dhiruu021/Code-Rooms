Origin of First Order Partial Differential Equations

Introduction to the origin of first-order partial differential equations:

  • Definition: A partial differential equation (PDE) involves multiple independent variables and their partial derivatives.
  • Origin: Derived from the application of physical laws in multi-dimensional systems, such as heat flow or fluid dynamics.
  • Example: Heat conduction in a rod can be modeled by a first-order PDE.

Partial Differential Equations of the First Order and Degree One

Understanding first-order PDEs of degree one:

  • Form: A general first-order PDE is written as F(x, y, p) = 0, where p = ∂u/∂x and u is the dependent variable.
  • Solution: Techniques like the method of characteristics are used to solve such equations.
  • Example: ∂u/∂x + ∂u/∂y = 0, representing a wave-like equation.

Lagrange’s Solution

Understanding Lagrange’s solution to first-order PDEs:

  • Definition: Lagrange's method is used for solving first-order linear partial differential equations.
  • Solution Approach: The solution involves finding a general solution of the form φ(x, y, u) = C, where C is a constant.
  • Example: ∂u/∂x + ∂u/∂y = 0, solved using the method of characteristics and Lagrange's approach.

Partial Differential Equations of First Order and Degree Greater than One

Solving first-order PDEs of degree greater than one:

  • Form: PDEs of the form F(x, y, u, p, q) = 0, where p = ∂u/∂x and q = ∂u/∂y.
  • Solution Approach: Involves solving using the method of Charpit's equations or the Lagrange method, depending on the degree.
  • Example: F(x, y, u, ∂u/∂x, ∂u/∂y) = 0, a typical form of first-order PDEs of higher degree.

Solution of Second Order Linear Partial Differential Equations with Constant Coefficients

Solving second-order linear PDEs with constant coefficients:

  • Form: A typical second-order linear PDE is ∂²u/∂x² + ∂²u/∂y² = 0, known as Laplace’s equation.
  • Solution Method: The solution involves using separation of variables or transforming the equation into simpler forms.
  • Example: The heat conduction equation ∂u/∂t = α∇²u, a second-order linear PDE.
